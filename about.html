About Us

Supporting student mental health through community, awareness, and care.

Our Mission

The University of Toronto Mississauga Mental Health Student Association (UTM MHSA) was founded in 2021 to support and empower UTM students on their mental health journeys. We connect students with both on-campus and off-campus mental health resources while fostering awareness of mental wellness among students and staff alike.

At its core, UTM MHSA is about building a supportive community where individuals feel encouraged to share their personal experiences with mental health and well-being. Like a butterfly spreading its wings, we hope to inspire growth, resilience, and transformation in every student we reach.

What We Do

Support & Awareness

We create a safe space for conversations surrounding mental health, helping reduce stigma and encourage open dialogue within the UTM community.

Events & Workshops

Through interactive events, wellness activities, and educational workshops, we promote mental well-being in engaging and accessible ways.

Community Building

Our goal is to foster a welcoming environment where students feel supported during stressful times such as midterms, exams, and personal challenges.

Our Vision

We understand that university life can make it challenging to seek help for mental health struggles. That’s why we host interactive events, facilitate engaging discussions, and bring attention to the mental health issues most relevant to our community.

Our goal is to continue growing the association into a safe and welcoming space where all UTM students can find support during stressful times—whether it’s midterms, exams, or personal challenges. By fostering these conversations, we aim to break the stigma surrounding mental health and invite all students to join us in making this vision a reality.
